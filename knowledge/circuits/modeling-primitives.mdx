---
title: Modeling Primitives
description: Shared circuit laws and numerical representations for Greybound circuit cells.
---

These primitives are the default language for reusable circuit cells. They keep physical behavior explicit enough to validate without forcing every model to become a full circuit simulator.

## Conservation Laws

Use Kirchhoff's Current Law for nodal solves and Kirchhoff's Voltage Law when writing loop or port equations. A circuit model should identify:

- node voltages or port waves,
- branch currents through capacitors, resistors, sources, and nonlinear devices,
- external source and load impedances,
- control elements that change topology or component values.

If a proposed DSP block cannot identify the voltage or current it represents, treat it as an approximation and validate it against AC, transient, and audio references before accepting it as physical.

## Equivalent Sources

Use Thevenin or Norton equivalents at model boundaries when a full upstream/downstream solve is unnecessary. Document:

- source voltage or current,
- source impedance,
- expected load impedance range,
- frequency-dependent elements such as cable capacitance or coupling capacitors.

Do not collapse source and load impedance into a static gain unless the target circuit is insensitive to that loading or the simplification has measured support.

## Modified Nodal Analysis

MNA is the preferred representation for passive networks and small mixed linear/nonlinear cells with meaningful impedance interaction.

For each MNA cell, document:

- node list and reference ground,
- dynamic state variables,
- sample-rate-dependent conductance terms,
- source/load ports,
- control-position mapping,
- solver fallback for singular or near-singular configurations.

Use trapezoidal integration for capacitors by default. Backward Euler can be used when stability is more important than exact high-frequency behavior, but the damping tradeoff must be noted.

## Wave Digital Filters

WDFs are appropriate when a circuit can be decomposed into ports with stable adaptors and local nonlinearities. Prefer WDF when it gives better numerical robustness or cleaner modular composition than a direct MNA solve.

Document port resistances, adaptor topology, nonlinear root solve, and how external impedance changes are handled. Avoid WDF abstractions that hide observable node behavior needed for validation.

## State-Space Blocks

State-space models are acceptable for validated linear subcircuits, especially fixed tone shaping or output filtering. They must include the source/load assumptions used to derive the matrices.

If controls alter the network, either derive a controlled state-space family or use an MNA/WDF formulation instead of interpolating unrelated filters without validation.

## Acceptance Criteria

A primitive-level model is acceptable when:

- all energy-storing elements have explicit state,
- external impedance assumptions are stated,
- DC operating point is explainable,
- AC response can be compared against reference curves,
- transient behavior remains stable under silence, overload, and control changes.

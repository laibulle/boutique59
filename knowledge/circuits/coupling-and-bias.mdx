---
title: Coupling And Bias
description: Shared rules for coupling capacitors, bias networks, and blocking behavior.
---

Coupling and bias networks create memory that strongly affects recovery, feel, and inter-stage loading. Keep this state explicit.

## Coupling Capacitors

A coupling capacitor should be modeled at the boundary where it exists electrically. Document:

- series capacitor value,
- grid/base/input leak resistance,
- source impedance,
- downstream input impedance,
- initial condition,
- discharge path when bypassed or disconnected.

The simple high-pass cutoff is only a first check. Large-signal behavior depends on bias shift and downstream nonlinear conduction.

## Bias Networks

Bias assumptions must be visible in the model plan:

- fixed or cathode/emitter bias,
- grid/base leak path,
- cathode/emitter resistor and bypass capacitor,
- supply voltage assumption,
- expected DC operating point.

When the true operating point is unknown, mark it as a measurement or SPICE requirement rather than hiding it in a gain constant.

## Blocking Distortion

Blocking behavior can occur when coupling capacitors charge under overload and shift the following device bias. It matters most in tube stages and high-gain cascades.

Model it when the target behavior includes:

- slow recovery after hard transients,
- low-frequency compression or choking,
- asymmetric recovery,
- audible dependence on coupling-capacitor values.

At minimum, add validation stimuli with low-frequency bursts and high-amplitude transients when blocking behavior is in scope.

## Bypass Capacitors

Cathode and emitter bypass capacitors alter gain and introduce memory. Keep their state separate from static gain.

Document:

- bypass capacitor value,
- resistor path,
- sample-rate discretization,
- effect on small-signal gain,
- overload recovery behavior.

## Tests

Coupling and bias tests should cover:

- DC settling from cold state,
- silence after overload,
- low-frequency sine response,
- burst recovery,
- control changes that alter discharge paths.

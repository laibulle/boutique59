---
title: Tone Stacks
description: Reusable modeling rules for passive and active tone-control networks.
---

Tone stacks are interactive impedance networks. Treat them as circuit cells unless measurements show that a simpler filter bank is equivalent for the target use.

## Passive Stack Rules

Passive tone stacks should include:

- source impedance from the driving stage,
- downstream load impedance,
- all tone capacitors and resistors,
- potentiometers as split resistors,
- taper mapping or documented taper assumption,
- insertion loss.

Do not normalize away insertion loss unless the surrounding amplifier stage includes the real makeup gain.

## Common Topologies

Fender/Marshall-style three-knob stacks:

- slope resistor and treble capacitor dominate mid/treble interaction,
- bass and mid controls strongly affect insertion loss,
- source impedance matters, especially when driven by a cathode follower.

Vox/Top Boost-style stacks:

- treble and bass controls interact through a small passive network,
- upstream cathode-follower or plate-source impedance changes response,
- downstream loading changes level and high-frequency shape.

Muffin-style blend tone networks:

- low-pass and high-pass branches should be solved as one loaded blend,
- blend pot position changes both level and effective frequency response,
- recovery-stage input load is part of the cell.

## Implementation Preference

Use MNA for interactive passive tone stacks. A state-space or biquad approximation can be used only after fitting and validating:

- control endpoints,
- mid control positions,
- source/load variants,
- insertion loss,
- phase response when it affects downstream clipping or feedback.

## Validation Sweeps

For each tone stack, keep reference AC sweeps for:

- all controls minimum, noon, maximum,
- each control swept while others are fixed,
- realistic source/load values,
- at least 44.1 kHz and 48 kHz discrete implementations when product builds support both.

Rust fixtures should compare magnitude at fixed frequencies and reject unstable or singular control positions.

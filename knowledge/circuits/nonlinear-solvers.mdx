---
title: Nonlinear Solvers
description: Shared solver rules for diodes, BJTs, tubes, and other nonlinear circuit cells.
---

Nonlinear circuit behavior should be solved where the nonlinearity interacts with surrounding impedance. A generic waveshaper is only appropriate when the circuit interaction is not material or has been validated as an equivalent.

## Default Solver Shape

Use small bounded Newton iterations for scalar and low-dimensional nonlinear cells:

- start from the previous sample's solution,
- limit iteration count,
- clamp exponential arguments,
- bound voltage/current updates,
- provide a deterministic fallback if convergence fails,
- expose convergence counters in debug or lab builds when useful.

For audio-rate code, avoid heap allocation and data-dependent unbounded loops.

## Diode Clipping

Diode pairs should include the surrounding source impedance, series resistance, bias path, and capacitive state when those elements affect clipping threshold or recovery.

Document:

- diode equation or approximation,
- thermal voltage and emission coefficient assumptions,
- forward voltage confidence,
- symmetric or asymmetric device configuration,
- coupling with tone or feedback networks.

Lookup tables or polynomial approximations are acceptable after comparison against the reference equation over the expected voltage range.

## BJT Cells

BJT stages should preserve operating-point behavior, gain dependence on collector/emitter networks, coupling capacitors, and feedback paths.

Document:

- topology and transistor orientation,
- collector, base, and emitter network values,
- beta or transconductance assumption,
- bias point,
- nonlinear relation used for base-emitter behavior,
- source and load impedance.

When exact device data is unavailable, state the placeholder parameter and validate the audible sensitivity before treating it as a stable project value.

## Tube Cells

Tube stages should preserve asymmetric transfer, bias shift, cathode memory, and loading. Compact triode equations are acceptable when their parameter ranges are documented.

Document:

- triode type and model family,
- plate, grid, and cathode networks,
- plate supply assumption,
- cathode bypass state,
- coupling-capacitor state,
- downstream load and tone-stack interaction.

For cathode followers and phase inverters, validate both signal transfer and output impedance behavior because their main role is often driving another network.

## Solver Validation

Every nonlinear solver should have tests for:

- silence and denormal-safe decay,
- small-signal gain around the operating point,
- overload recovery,
- DC bias stability,
- harmonic profile at representative input levels,
- behavior at sample rates used by the product.

When SPICE is available, compare node voltages and branch currents before comparing final audio.
